Research Focus

My work focuses on workplace mental health for vulnerable neurodivergent emerging adults. I use predominantly qualitative methodologies with creative approaches, including work with edible sculptures and paintings on edible materials to fill critical gaps in mental health support literature.

Background

Originally from Oxfordshire and now residing in the West Midlands, I hold a BA in Media Production from Coventry University. My diverse background includes extensive experience in events, photography, publishing, training, and education. I am a trained secondary teacher specializing in children with complex communication difficulties.

As a neurodivergent person raising a neurodivergent family, I bring personal insight and passion to my research on neurodivergent workplace experiences.

Current Research

Innovative approaches to understanding workplace mental health

Creative Qualitative Methodologies

My current research employs innovative creative methodologies, including the use of edible materials within qualitative research. This approach, influenced by local employability training programs in central Birmingham, aims to build deeper understanding of lived experiences within qualitative research frameworks.

Research Goals

  • Fill gaps in mental health support literature for young, vulnerable adults
  • Create a comprehensive toolkit for workplace mental health
  • Develop accessible recruitment and research methodologies
  • Support neurodivergent emerging adults in workplace environments
