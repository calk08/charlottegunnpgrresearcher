Research Overview

Innovative approaches to understanding and supporting neurodivergent emerging adults in workplace environments

Primary Research Focus

Workplace Mental Health for Vulnerable Neurodivergent Emerging Adults

My PhD research addresses a critical gap in the literature surrounding mental health support for young, vulnerable adults in workplace environments. Specifically, I focus on neurodivergent emerging adults (ages 18-30) who face unique challenges in professional settings.

Key Research Questions

  • How do neurodivergent emerging adults experience mental health challenges in workplace environments?
  • What support mechanisms are most effective for this vulnerable population?
  • How can creative methodologies enhance our understanding of lived experiences?
  • What tools and resources can be developed to improve workplace mental health support?

Research Goals & Objectives

01

Fill Literature Gap

Address the shortage of research on mental health support for young, vulnerable adults in workplace settings.

02

Develop Toolkit

Create comprehensive resources for workplace mental health support specifically designed for neurodivergent emerging adults.

03

Innovative Methods

Pioneer creative methodologies that make research more accessible and engaging for vulnerable populations.

04

Practical Application

Ensure research findings translate into practical, implementable workplace support strategies.

Creative Research Methodology

Innovative qualitative approaches using edible materials and creative methodologies

Innovative Approach

My research employs creative qualitative methodologies that go beyond traditional interview and survey methods. Central to this approach is the use of edible materials within qualitative research frameworks—a pioneering methodology that creates more engaging and accessible research experiences for participants.

Edible Art Methodology

Participants create edible sculptures and paintings on edible materials as a means of expressing their workplace experiences and mental health challenges. This approach:

  • Reduces traditional power dynamics in research settings
  • Provides alternative communication methods for participants
  • Creates tangible outputs that can be consumed and shared
  • Offers therapeutic benefits through creative expression

Participant-Centered Design

Research protocols are designed with accessibility and participant comfort as primary considerations, influenced by local employability training programs in central Birmingham:

  • Flexible session structures adapted to individual needs
  • Multiple communication options (verbal, visual, written)
  • Sensory-friendly environments
  • Collaborative meaning-making processes

Trauma-Informed Approach

Recognizing the vulnerability of the target population, all methodologies incorporate trauma-informed principles:

  • Safety and trustworthiness in all interactions
  • Participant choice and control over their involvement
  • Cultural humility and responsiveness
  • Collaboration and mutuality in research relationships

Why Creative Methodologies?

Enhanced Accessibility

Creative methods accommodate diverse communication styles and cognitive differences common in neurodivergent populations.

Reduced Barriers

Non-traditional approaches help overcome anxiety and hesitation often associated with formal research participation.

Richer Data

Participants can express complex experiences that may be difficult to articulate through words alone.

Empowerment

Participants become co-creators of knowledge rather than passive subjects of study.

Core Accessibility Principles

Creating accessible participant information sheets is a fundamental responsibility when working with vulnerable groups. My approach incorporates evidence-based design principles to ensure information is truly accessible to all potential participants.

Visual Accessibility

Design Considerations:
  • Font Selection: Clear, sans-serif fonts (minimum 12pt)
  • Color Contrast: High contrast ratios (minimum 4.5:1)
  • Line Spacing: Increased spacing for improved readability
  • White Space: Adequate margins and spacing to reduce cognitive load
  • Consistent Layout: Predictable structure and navigation

Language Accessibility

Communication Strategies:
  • Plain Language: Avoiding jargon and technical terms
  • Short Sentences: Clear, concise expression of ideas
  • Active Voice: Direct, straightforward communication
  • Bullet Points: Breaking down complex information
  • Definitions: Explaining necessary technical terms

Visual Communication

Supportive Visual Elements:
  • Infographics: Visual representation of key concepts
  • Icons: Universal symbols to support understanding
  • Flowcharts: Clear process visualization
  • Alt Text: Descriptive text for all images
  • Consistent Imagery: Cohesive visual language throughout

Implementation Examples

Traditional Approach
"Participants will be required to complete comprehensive demographic questionnaires and engage in semi-structured interviews lasting approximately 60-90 minutes, which will be audio-recorded for subsequent transcription and thematic analysis."
Accessible Approach
What will I be asked to do?
• Fill out a short form about yourself
• Talk with the researcher for 1-1.5 hours
• Your conversation will be recorded
• You can take breaks anytime
Complex Consent
"By signing below, you acknowledge that you have read and understood the information provided and consent to participate in this research study with full knowledge of the potential risks and benefits."
Clear Consent
I understand:
✓ What the study is about
✓ What I will be asked to do
✓ I can leave at any time
✓ My information will be kept private
